Coronaviruses (CoV) are a wide family of viruses
that cause illnesses ranging from the common
cold to life-threatening disorders. A novel
coronavirus (nCoV) is a strain of coronavirus that
has not before been discovered in humans.
The discovered virus was later named "COVID-19
virus."

The most common symptoms of COVID-19,
according to the WHO, are fever, fatigue, and a
dry cough. A few individuals report aches and
pains, nasal congestion, a runny nose, a sore
throat, or diarrhea.
